Annoy your MP

Violence against women and girls is more than an epidemic in Trinidad and Tobago. It is a part of our deep-seated patriarchal value system that disempowers women and girls and subjects them to gratuitous harm. In Trinidad and Tobago, over 2019-2024, a total of 16,755 reports were made for various domestic violence offences. Of that number, only 7.3% persons charged and only 1.2% actually convicted/prosecuted. This means that the proportion of conviction/prosecution to total number of reports from 2019 to 2024 is 0.01 (see more stats here). It is heavy and enraging to say that the case of Mercedes Cabrera-Layne, may her soul rest peacefully, is neither new nor unfamiliar.

Her experiences now exist as our more recent public example of how our lives are at threat both in private and in public, spectacularly and in the mundane. Mercedes was on her way home when she disappeared and her body was later found. Transportation should not mean death. Our lives are not disposable. We are not powerless.

As a community, our collective power has the capacity to mobilise for change in the form of stronger legislation as well as increased public education about gender based violence and community response models. Until then there are low-effort actions that you, your family members and friends can take to agitate the state towards greater action: annoy your MP.

We understand that doing so might seem daunting so we’ve created a template and script that you can use when you call, write or speak to your Member of Parliament.

Email Text

Dear Hon. [MP Name here],

I am [name here], living in [area]. The recent news of Mercedes Cabrera-Layne is among a string of similar occurrences of young girls being murdered. Gender-based violence continues to be a threat to the safety and wellbeing of young girls and, by extension, a public health crisis.

I believe that our legislation and policies need to be strengthened to safeguard the lives of the most vulnerable people in our society: children.

As a member of your constituency, I expect to hear you speak up in parliament and the media supporting stronger legislative protections and better public education on the eradication of gender based violence.
